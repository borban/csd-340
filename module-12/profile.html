Ben Orban's Profile

CACI

I currently work as a Software Engineer for a federal contractor CACI, Inc. The program I work for is based in Scott Air Force Base in southern Illinois and is part of USTRANSCOM. This program is where I initially made the switch from system administrator to software engineer and I have worked for over 3 years on this program. Albeit on 3 separate tours. I enjoy working on software that directly benefits members of the military and their families during a time when it might be most stressful.



Enterprise Holdings

I worked for 4 years total for Enterprise Holdings. Three years were spent working on their main operations application called ECARS. This is the software that all the branches for Enterprise, Alamo, and National used for their daily rental operations. I spent one year working on the hourly rental CarShare program that was placed in multiple large cities. I gained a lot of knowledge during the four years with the company and was promoted from associate engineer to mid-level engineer in that time.



Savvis

I worked for one year as a System Administrator at Savvis. This company was bought out by Centurylink right before I moved on to work at USTRANSCOM. Savvis was a web hosting provider in direct competition with the likes of Amazon Web Services and others. I was responsible for helping support multiple Fortune 500 companies maintain their websites and provide multiple services such as certificate management, packaging and deploying updates, and providing triage and remediation services when sites had problems.

Golf

Golf is a recent hobby I have picked up over the last few years. I find it to be both challenging and relaxing at the same time. I grew up in more of a rural setting, so the courses allow me to enjoy the landscapes and be in nature while the game challenges me mentally and physically.



Space

Since I was a young child, space as the final frontier always fascinated me. In part, my father and grandfather helped build this interest through the constant talk of the space shuttle and NASA. I keep watch on current industry advancements by reading articles, following Twitter, and also like to watch launches. The recent news with the James Webb Telescope is absolutely stunning and I look forward to all the scientific data that will come out from this first of it's kind observatory.



Travel

Traveling is something that always made me feel alive. I think it is ingrained in me because my father and his family were military brats and were always on the move and this wanderlust was passed down to me. Although, I did not travel out of the country until I was in my 20's, I had done a good amount of traveling domestically in the U.S. This is a hobby that I share with my wife, and as a result, we went on a road-trip to the Grand Canyon as our honeymoon.
